Brand Introduction

For over a century, Coca-Cola has been a simple invitation to slow down, share a moment, and open up a bottle of happiness. Rooted in a timeless heritage of warmth and authenticity, we believe that the smallest moments of connection can bridge any gap. Whether shared at a retro diner counter or a modern family table, our purpose remains unchanged: to refresh the world, inspire moments of optimism, and foster a sense of belonging. Guided by our commitment to integrity and community, we are dedicated to crafting a sustainable future that honors our past while looking forward to a brighter, shared tomorrow. Welcome to our table—let's share a moment together.

Core Values

Integrity

Honest, transparent, and accountable. We hold ourselves to the highest standards of quality, ensuring every bottle and interaction lives up to our heritage.

How it shows up: Publicly sharing nutritional info and environmental goals with clear, accessible metrics.

Innovation

Forward-thinking and solution-oriented, not afraid to evolve. While we honor our history, we constantly seek smarter, more sustainable ways to package, distribute, and share.

How it shows up: Launching 100% recycled PET bottles and investing in carbon-neutral delivery fleets.

Respect for Community

Inclusive, listening, and giving back. We design spaces and experiences that bring people together, actively supporting local communities and initiatives.

How it shows up: Funding local park restoration programs and community recycling hubs globally.

Logo Direction

Signature Logo Mark

Logo Brief & Specifications

  • Recommended Mark Type

    Combination Mark containing the flowing Spencerian script wordmark paired with the iconic dynamic contour ribbon (the wave) for fluid movement and energy.

  • Stylistic Approach

    Timeless Heritage with Modern Clarity. Preserve the retro script for brand equity but apply clean padding constraints (minimum padding equal to the letter C) for mobile screens.

  • Iconography Direction

    The dynamic contour ribbon serves as the secondary mark. A clean vector silhouette of the classic contour glass bottle can be used as a background watermark or graphic detail.

  • Usage Versatility

    Must operate in single-color Red on White background or White on solid Red background. Do not place logo script directly over complex multi-color photography.

Tone of Voice

The Vocal Style

Our voice acts as a warm, optimistic, and welcoming host. We speak with the authentic familiarity of an old friend, keeping our messaging clean, simple, and down-to-earth while inspiring small moments of everyday joy.

Optimistic & Uplifting

Highlighting the positive and focusing on moments of shared happiness.

Do: "Every day holds a reason to smile; let's find it together."
Don't: "Maximize your satisfaction levels with our carbonated drink."

Warm & Inclusive

Speaking to everyone, bringing different generations and communities closer.

Do: "Pull up a chair and share a cold one with us."
Don't: "Yo, grab a drink right now, it's lit."

Simple & Direct

Avoiding marketing fluff and overly scientific or complex jargon.

Do: "Real taste. Simple ingredients. Pure refreshment."
Don't: "A synergistic formulation designed for beverage hydration optimization."

Brand Guidelines

Usage Do's

  • ✓
    Background Consistency

    Use Vintage Cream as the primary canvas background color to maintain warmth and nostalgia.

  • ✓
    Logo Padding

    Keep a clear space equal to the height of the letter "C" in the Coca-Cola script around the entire logo.

  • ✓
    Header Typography

    Use Montserrat Bold/Black for display titles to ensure clean, high-impact readability.

  • ✓
    Single-Color Logo Contrast

    Use the solid Classic White logo on solid Coca-Cola Red backgrounds for maximum accessibility.

Usage Don'ts

  • ✗
    Logo Distortion

    Never stretch, rotate, skew, or apply complex drop shadows to the script logo wordmark.

  • ✗
    Red Body Canvas

    Do not use Coca-Cola Red as a background color for large sections of body text, as it degrades readability.

  • ✗
    Font Mixing

    Do not introduce off-palette fonts or display scripts. Stick strictly to Montserrat and Inter.

  • ✗
    Logo on Photo Textures

    Do not overlay the script logo directly onto complex or busy photographic textures without a backing card.
